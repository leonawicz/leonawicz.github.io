Statistician | Statistical Programmer | useR

Scenarios Network for Alaska & Arctic Planning

I am the Lead Statistical Analyst for Scenarios Network for Alaska & Arctic Planning at the University of Alaska Fairbanks. I love programming in R. Much of my work revolves around high performance statistical computing on large data sets, often including parallel processing on multi-node Linux clusters, sometimes in conjunction with other technologies such as Open MPI.

I develop statistical web applications for presentation of statistical analyses and scientific information suitable for a range of audiences. I have a strong interest in data visualization as well and always enjoy opportunities to create informative, beautiful graphics. I'm also a huge fan of dynamic report generation (Rmarkdown and knitr) and other tools like Shiny for project documentation and presentation.

I play a leading role in developing and promoting scientific and analytic coding best practices for SNAP. I also lead actively in development and deployment of new analytic and visualization tools, generating scientific interest and financial support for new projects. R package: mapmate v 0.1.0 available on Github

The mapmate package is used for map- and globe-based data animation pre-production. Specifically, mapmate functions are used to generate and save to disk a series of map graphics that make up a still image sequence, which can then be used in video editing and rendering software of the user's choice.

mapmate on Github.

Trump Goggles is an Android app that leverages the analytics power of R to categorize, display and summarize the emotions in a sample of tweets by Donald Trump based on a sentiment analysis of his Twitter feed. Get the app.
